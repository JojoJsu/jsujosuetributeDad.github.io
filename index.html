LEONARD CHUKWUMA UGWUANYI

By Joshua S. Ugwuanyi.

March 8, 2024

Late Chief Leonard Chukwuma Ugwuanyi passed unto greater glory on Saturday, the 4th of May 2019. Chukwuma was born to Late Agu-Nkpozi Ojoba Ugwuanyi and his wife, late Obuloko Theresa Ugwuanyi on November 25, 1962 in Apkanya, Kogi State, Nigeria. Both parents hailed from Obukpa in Nsukka Central, Enugu State, Nigeria. Chukwuma, was therefore, a Nigerian citizen by birth, and of Igbo origin, specifically Nsukka Asadu Ideke Arumona extraction.

Chukwuma was a practicing catholic and was honoured in 2014 by the college of patrons of the catholic artistes and entertainers association of Nigeria Abuja for his numerous contributions in the lord’s vineyard. Chukwuma was the first son in a polygamous family consisting of two wives, five sons and five daughters. He was raised and supported through his university education by late Mr Silvester Ngwoke (One Nigeria) of Amugwu Obukpa, Nsukka, Nigeria. He attended Boys High School, Ekposhi Enugu Ezike, Igbo Eze North L.G.A. Enugu State between 1976 – 1981, University of Nigeria, Nsukka for his bachelor’s degree in Mathematics Education between 1984 – 1988, University of Jos for his master programme in Tests and Measurement between (1989 – 1992) and Nasarawa State University, Keffi where he was about concluding his PhD in Measurement and Evaluation.

Chukwuma had a brilliant career in the civil service, rising in 2017 to the coveted post of Director in the National Commission for Colleges of Education, Abuja. In the world of work, he had 30 years of teaching experience, 30 years of creative active research, 20 years of project management and 15 years of consultancy locally and internationally. He also had over 30 significant publications to his name, attended over 12 local and international conferences, seminars and workshops. He has commissioned six training workshops with STAN and NIPR He made significant contribution in various Mathematical Games and Simulations projects in National Mathematical Centre Abuja. He contributed in the Ludo – Stat Mathematical Game, Nzogbu – Nzogbu Enyimba Mathematical Game, Dart a Name Mathematical Game, Spine\ner Algebra Game, Mathematics Relay and Arithmatic Tic. Tac Times. He successfully completed 11 research projects in the National Commission for Colleges of Education Abuja some which involved UNESCO and World Bank. He was appointed into various positions in NMC, STAN, Nigerian Institute of Public Relation,

He acted as, member, resource person, Editor-in-Chief, zonal co-ordinator, chairman for (STAN) Mathematics Subject Panel (2003 to 2019), Resource person (NIPR) 1993, Plateau State Co-ordinator (APQEN) 1994, (NCNE), Research Committee Secretary NCCE 1999 to 2019, Federal delegates to SMASE project in Kenya and South Africa (2004), Kenya (2007), Facilitator ETF/NCCE project, Head/Desk Officer, SMASE-Nigeria INSET Centre NCCE, Abuja, Master Trainer FLEHI and ECCE (2008-2019), Facilitator/Resource Person, Mac-Arthur/NCCE/UNESCO Project (2007 – 2019). INSET System Construction, CEMASTEA, Kenya, Pedagogy for Primary Education JICA-Sappporo, Japan, He was appointed as council member to FCE, Abeokuta, (2008-2011), FCET Omoku, (2012 – 2015), Adeyemi College of Education, Ondo (2014-2017) and FCET Asaba. He was a member of management board of many institutions.

In the community service, he was the chairman, Igbo Community Welfare Association Zone 34 Asikolaye, Kaduna State 1999 to 2001. Part-Time/Associate Lecturer, Faculty of Education, University of Jos, 1989 – 1995. Secretary, STAN Mathematics Subject Panel (1999 – 2004). Chairman, STAN Mathematics Subject Panel (2005 – 2010). Patrons to many association.

Chukwuma received the Obukpa chieftaincy title in 2013.

Chukwuma’s life is reflected in the composition of his many friends. Any attempt to name all of them could impact invidiously on the many who might be inadvertently omitted. Suffice to say his friends came from every part of Nigeria and other part of the world.
